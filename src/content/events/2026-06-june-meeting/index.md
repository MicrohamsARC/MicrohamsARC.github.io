## Program

### Field Day 2026 Planning — N7OS at Camp Freeman

This month's meeting is all about **[ARRL Field Day 2026](/events/2026-06-arrl-field-day)**, our biggest operating event of the year. MicroHAMS returns to **Camp Freeman near Monroe** to operate as **N7OS** the weekend of **June 27–28**, with setup starting Friday afternoon, June 26.

We'll walk through the plan and lock in the details together:

- **The site:** what Camp Freeman offers — covered pavilion, camping and limited RV/trailer space, AC power for the kitchen, and solar power for the radios via the MITRU
- **The stations:** our 3A setup (three band-dedicated HF transmitters) plus a VHF station for 6m, 2m, and 70cm, with computer logging and propagation mapping, supported by Snohomish County ACS / Emergency Management resources
- **Volunteer assignments:** antenna raising, bonus-point activities (satellite contacts, copying the W1AW bulletin, sending NTS messages), computers and networking, greeters, and parking
- **Logistics:** camping plans, who's bringing trailers or RVs, food, and the setup/teardown timeline

Whether you're a seasoned contester or have never operated Field Day before, come find out how to take part. There's a job — and a seat at a radio — for everyone.

**Coordinating Field Day:** Grant Hopper, KB7WSD. Reach him at [kb7wsd@gmail.com](mailto:kb7wsd@gmail.com), and join the Field Day group at [groups.google.com/g/microhamsfd](https://groups.google.com/g/microhamsfd) to get in on the planning.
